A good painter can add serious value to your property. A bad one can leave you with peeling paint and legal headaches. These questions will help you tell the difference.

## 1. Are You Licensed and Insured?

Any painter doing significant domestic work should be able to show licences and public liability insurance.

## 2. What Preparation Is Included?

Surface preparation is where cheap quotes cut corners. Get details in writing.

## 3. What Products Do You Use?

Professional painters use well‑known brands that stand behind their products.

## 4. What Warranty Do You Offer?

Look for a clear workmanship warranty in writing, not just verbal promises.
