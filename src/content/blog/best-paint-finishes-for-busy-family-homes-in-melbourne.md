## Why Paint Finish Matters More Than People Think

Finish changes the way paint behaves.

It affects:

- washability  
- durability  
- light reflection  
- how imperfections show  
- how the room feels overall  

In family homes across Melbourne, this matters a lot more than on a sample board in a quiet showroom.

## Flat and Matte Finishes

Flat and matte finishes are good at hiding imperfections. They create a soft look that works well on ceilings and calmer rooms.

They are useful where you want:

- low reflectivity  
- softer visual texture  
- better hiding of imperfect plaster  

They are less useful where sticky hands frequently operate.

## Low Sheen and Washable Wall Finishes

This is often the sweet spot for family homes.

Low sheen or washable matte finishes give you:

- better cleanability than flat  
- a softer look than shinier options  
- a practical balance for most wall surfaces  

For homes in suburbs like Glen Waverley, Bentleigh, Doncaster, or Reservoir where hallways, living areas, and kids’ bedrooms take real punishment, low sheen is often the most practical answer.

## Satin, Semi-Gloss, and Gloss

These finishes are stronger visually and physically.

They are often used on:

- trims  
- doors  
- frames  
- skirtings  
- cabinetry in some cases  

They are easier to wipe clean but also show more surface imperfections. On poorly prepared trim, a shinier finish will expose every shortcut with great enthusiasm.

## Best Finishes for Each Room

### Living rooms
Low sheen is usually the most balanced option.

### Hallways
Durable washable finishes matter here because hallways collect impacts, bags, marks, and traffic.

### Kids’ bedrooms
Washable low sheen or a durable matte designed for cleaning makes sense.

### Ceilings
Flat is still the usual choice because it hides imperfections best.

### Trim and doors
Semi-gloss or gloss often works best for durability and wipeability.

## What Families Usually Regret

Common regrets include:

- choosing flat paint on high-traffic walls  
- using ultra-glossy finishes on average plaster  
- matching all surfaces with one finish for simplicity  
- underestimating how often hallways and skirtings get touched  

A finish that looks elegant in theory can become annoying in practice if it does not match the way the home is used.

## A Real Example From a Family Home

A family with three children repainted their Melbourne home and initially wanted a soft matte finish on every wall because they liked the look online. After talking through how the house was actually used, they changed the plan.

Ceilings stayed flat. Bedroom walls got a durable low sheen. Hallways and living areas used a washable wall finish. Trims were done in semi-gloss.

The result still looked calm and modern, but the house was much easier to live with. Which is the point.

## Final Thoughts

The **best paint finish for a busy family home in Melbourne** is usually not the most fashionable one. It is the one that survives family life without making you resent the walls.

And that pristine room from the start? It stays pristine for about eight minutes unless the finish was chosen with reality in mind.

Better to paint for the home you actually live in.

## FAQ: Paint Finishes for Family Homes

### What is the best paint finish for family room walls?
Usually a low sheen or washable wall finish because it balances durability and appearance well.

### Is flat paint a bad idea for kids’ rooms?
It can be less practical because it is usually harder to clean than more washable finishes.

### What finish is best for trims and doors?
Semi-gloss or gloss are common because they are tougher and easier to wipe clean.

### Does shinier paint show more defects?
Yes. The more reflective the finish, the more it highlights uneven surfaces and preparation flaws.

### Can one paint finish work for the whole house?
It can, but it is rarely the smartest choice. Different rooms and surfaces usually need different levels of durability and reflectivity.
