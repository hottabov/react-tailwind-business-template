It is easy to underestimate the cost of a full repaint, especially when you only see the per‑litre price of paint on a shelf. This checklist helps you budget like a pro.

## 1. Surfaces to Be Painted

List every area: walls, ceilings, doors, frames, skirting, windows, built‑ins. The more detailed your list, the more accurate your quote.

## 2. Repairs and Preparation

Allow a line item for plaster repairs, gap filling and sanding. Older homes almost always need more prep.

## 3. Paint Quality and Finish

Better paints cover faster and last longer. A slightly higher material cost often saves labour.

## 4. Access and Timing

Steep driveways, tight stairwells or limited parking can add time to a job. So can strict noise restrictions in apartments.

## 5. Contingency

Add 10–15% contingency to your budget for unexpected repairs or colour changes. It is much less stressful than scrambling for extra funds later.
