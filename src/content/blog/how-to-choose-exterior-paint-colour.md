Choosing the wrong exterior paint colour is one of the most expensive home improvement mistakes you can make. Unlike an interior wall you can repaint on a whim, your exterior is a long‑term commitment that faces Melbourne's intense UV rays, rainy winters, and the scrutiny of every passer‑by.

## 1. Start With Architecture

A Federation‑style cottage loves soft heritage tones. A mid‑century brick home suits charcoals and off‑whites. Let the architecture narrow your options before you open the fan‑deck.

## 2. Consider the Street

You do not need to match your neighbours, but you should complement them. Aim for “best dressed on the street”, not “only one in costume”.

## 3. Understand Melbourne Light

Melbourne light is cool and soft compared to Queensland. Warm whites and greiges usually look better outside than very blue, crisp whites.

## 4. Always Test on the Wall

Sample pots belong on the actual wall, not on A4 paper. Paint at least an A3 patch and view it morning, midday, and afternoon.

## 5. Get Professional Help

If you are stuck between three or four options, a professional colour consultation is the fastest and safest way to make the right call.
