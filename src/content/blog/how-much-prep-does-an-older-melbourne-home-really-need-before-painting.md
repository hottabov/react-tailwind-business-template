You know that moment when someone says a house has “great bones,” and you immediately suspect that means you are about to pay for those bones? Older homes are like that. Full of charm. Full of detail. Full of stories. Also full of hairline cracks, patched plaster, old gloss coats, and small surprises hiding just beneath the surface.

When people ask how much preparation an **older Melbourne home** needs before painting, the honest answer is usually: more than they hoped, but less than a disaster if handled properly.

## Why Older Homes Need More Preparation

Older properties often have more surface history. More repaint cycles. More repairs. More movement. More areas where previous work was done “well enough at the time.”

In homes across Brunswick, Coburg, Northcote, Camberwell, or Essendon, painters often find layers of old decisions sitting under the current colour.

Preparation in these homes is usually heavier because the surfaces are less predictable.

## What Painters Usually Find in Older Houses

Common issues include:

- old patch repairs  
- hairline settling cracks  
- brittle caulking  
- flaking trim paint  
- nicotine or water staining  
- uneven plaster  
- gloss surfaces needing deglossing  
- gaps around architraves and skirtings  
- previous touch-ups flashing through in odd light  

None of this is unusual. It is just reality.

## Walls: The Hidden Story Beneath the Colour

On older walls, the colour you see is rarely the whole story.

A wall may look fine until:

- it is cleaned properly  
- light hits the patch repairs  
- old filler edges show up  
- loose paint begins lifting during prep  
- previous poor sanding becomes visible  

This is why older homes often need more filling, sanding, feathering, and spot priming than newer builds.

## Ceilings, Cornices, and Movement Cracks

Ceilings in older homes are often where movement tells its story.

Painters commonly deal with:

- fine cracks near cornices  
- patched ceiling joins  
- old water marks  
- flaky spots near previous leaks  
- uneven texture from past repairs  

Ceiling prep tends to be slower because defects catch light differently overhead. A rushed ceiling repair has a remarkable talent for announcing itself forever.

## Trim, Doors, and Old Gloss Build-Up

Older trims and doors often carry multiple generations of paint. Some were applied carefully. Some were clearly applied during moments of optimism and hurry.

Prep may involve:

- sanding back rough brush marks  
- filling dents and chips  
- smoothing built-up edges  
- removing loose sections  
- caulking gaps  
- spot priming worn areas  

This is where “just freshen the trim” can stop being a small sentence and become a larger number.

## Why Quotes Can Vary So Much

One painter may quote an older home assuming light prep. Another may price in the reality of what old surfaces usually demand.

That is why quotes vary.

The difference is not always greed. Sometimes it is simply one person pricing the visible job and another pricing the actual job.

## How to Budget for Prep Realistically

If the house is older, budget for:

- more labour  
- more sanding  
- more filling  
- more caulking  
- more primer  
- some surprises once work begins  

A contingency helps. So does choosing a painter who explains what prep is included rather than hiding behind broad phrases.

## A Real Example From an Older Melbourne Home

A family home in Melbourne’s inner north looked ready for a straightforward interior repaint. On first glance, it was tidy. Well kept. Fairly recently decorated.

Once prep started, the walls told a different story.

There were old picture hook repairs, flashing from previous touch-ups, cracks above door frames, and trim with years of built-up enamel. None of it was catastrophic. All of it mattered.

The job still went well. But it went well because the prep matched the age of the home.

## Final Thoughts

So how much prep does an **older Melbourne home** really need before painting?

Usually, enough that pretending it is a quick modern repaint is a mistake. Older homes reward careful preparation because the surfaces have history, and history always finds a way to show through paint if you ignore it.

And that “great bones” line from the start? It is often true. But bones still need skin that is prepared properly.

## FAQ: Prep for Older Homes

### Do older homes need more prep before painting?
Usually, yes. They often have more cracks, old repairs, gloss build-up, and surface inconsistency.

### Why do older house painting quotes vary so much?
Because some painters price for visible surfaces only, while others allow for the heavier prep older homes often require.

### Can paint hide old wall repairs?
Not reliably. Poorly prepared repairs often show through even fresh paint.

### Do older trims need special prep?
They often need more sanding, filling, and smoothing due to older gloss layers and wear.

### Should I budget extra for an older home repaint?
Yes. A contingency is smart because surprises are more common once preparation begins.
