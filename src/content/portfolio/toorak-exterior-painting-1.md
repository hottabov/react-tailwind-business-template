This wasn’t a repaint. This was a rescue mission.

A classic Federation home in **Toorak (~220 m² facade area)**, layered with at least three generations of bad decisions — mismatched whites, cracking trims, and paint that came off if you looked at it long enough.

## What We Found

- Peeling paint on 60% of exposed timber  
- Moisture damage near window trims  
- Previous painters skipped primer entirely  
- Hairline cracks across rendered sections  

The kind of job where you realise quickly: **painting is the easy part.**

## Scope & Timeline

- Total duration: **6 days**
- Prep work: **4 days**
- Painting: **2 days**
- Team: 2 painters

## Materials

- Dulux Weathershield (low sheen)
- Oil-based primer for timber restoration
- Flexible exterior filler for render cracks

## The Hard Part

Day 2 we hit a problem — large sections of timber fascia were soft.

Client thought it was “just cosmetic”.

It wasn’t.

We had to:
- Cut out damaged sections  
- Rebuild edges  
- Prime everything again  

That added a full extra day.

## Colour Decision

We avoided the typical bright white trend.

Instead:
- Warm off-white base  
- Deep charcoal trims  
- Muted green door  

Subtle, but expensive-looking.

## Result

The house didn’t just look newer — it looked intentional.

Neighbour walked over on day 5 and asked:
> “Who did your render?”

That’s when you know the paint job worked.
